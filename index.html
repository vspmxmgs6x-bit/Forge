90-DAY CARDIO PLAN
Goal: 18-min 5K · Current: ~26-min 5K (8:40/mi pace) · Target: 5:47/mi
PHASE 1 · WEEKS 1–4
AEROBIC BASE
Build your aerobic engine. Zone 2–3 steady state. Assault bike, rower, elliptical, and stair stepper all sub in on cross-training days. No pace pressure — consistency is the only goal.
PHASE 2 · WEEKS 5–9
THRESHOLD BUILD
Longer tempo runs, goal pace intervals. Stair stepper and elliptical on cross days — they hammer your cardiovascular system while keeping legs fresh for key run sessions.
PHASE 3 · WEEKS 10–13
RACE PREP
Race-pace intervals. Taper final week. Elliptical and pool used strategically to keep legs fresh. 5K time trial week 12 — measure your progress.
🫀 Zone guide for Matt (estimated max HR ~190 bpm): Zone 1: <114 Zone 2: 114–133 Zone 3: 133–152 Zone 4: 152–171 Zone 5: 171+ · Your WHOOP will refine these numbers over time.
YOUR EQUIPMENT — WHAT EACH DOES FOR YOU
🏃 Outdoor / Treadmill
Primary 5K training tool. Builds running economy and race-specific fitness. Treadmill lets you set exact pace — useful for goal pace intervals.
PRIORITY: HIGH for race goal
⚡ Assault Bike
Best VO2 max intervals. Full body — arms + legs. Can hit Zone 5 faster than running. Hard to cheat the effort. Brutal but highly effective for fat loss and cardiovascular adaptation.
PRIORITY: HIGH for VO2 max
🚣 Rowing Machine
Zero running impact. Full body aerobic work. Excellent for Zone 2–3 steady state. Builds posterior chain strength. Great option when legs are tired from lifting.
PRIORITY: HIGH for recovery days
🪜 Stair Stepper
Underrated fat-burner. Elevates HR quickly, works glutes/quads hard. Zone 3–4 without impact. For someone at your current fitness level, 25 min on the stepper is a serious cardiovascular workout. Great swap for assault bike.
PRIORITY: HIGH for fat loss
🔄 Elliptical
Lowest impact option. Good for Zone 2 steady state and active recovery. Use when legs are beat up from running or heavy lifts. Not ideal for VO2 max intervals but excellent for aerobic base volume.
PRIORITY: MED for recovery
🏊 Pool / Swimming
Best joint recovery. Full body aerobic demand with zero impact. If your joints ache or you're feeling beat up, swap any cross-training day for a 30 min easy swim. Also builds shoulder stability for your lifts.
PRIORITY: MED for recovery
